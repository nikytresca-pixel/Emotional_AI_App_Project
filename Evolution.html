EVOLUTION OF HUMAN–COMPUTER INTERACTION

"It is easy to design devices that work well when everything goes as planned. The hard and necessary part of design is to make things work well even when things do not go as planned."

Early Computing (1940s–1960s) Interaction was indirect and technical, based on batch processing and punched cards, accessible only to experts.

Command-Line Interfaces Users interacted with computers through textual commands, requiring memorization and technical knowledge.

Graphical User Interfaces (GUI) The introduction of windows, icons, menus, and pointers made computers more accessible to non-expert users.

Mobile and Touch Interaction Touchscreens and mobile devices enabled natural, direct interaction through gestures and mobility.

AI and Intelligent Interfaces Modern HCI focuses on adaptive, intelligent systems that understand users, context, and emotions.
