Early Human–Computer Interaction

Early computer systems relied on command-line interfaces, requiring users to interact through text-based commands and specialized knowledge.

Graphical User Interfaces

The introduction of graphical interfaces revolutionized HCI by enabling interaction through visual elements such as windows, icons, menus, and pointers.

Mobile and Touch Interaction

Touchscreens and mobile devices shifted interaction toward gestures, direct manipulation, and user-centered design.

The Future of HCI

Emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence, voice interfaces, and immersive environments are shaping more natural and adaptive interactions.
