Human Computer Interaction:
why is the user so important?

The central role of the user

Human-computer interaction (HCI) refers to a user-centered approach of design and development that optimizes synergy between human and computer. We can compare the HCI to a continous cycle, as each human action is followed by a response of the computer, and after that we act again.
Rather than designing technology for technology’s sake, HCI prioritizes the needs, goals, and limitations of the user. This involves extensive research into human factors, cognitive psychology, social sciences, and design principles.

Responsive design: ensuring a consistent user experience across all devices

Responsive design is an approach to web design which aims at allowing desktop pages to be viewed in response to the size of the screen, in order to facilitate usability, navigation and information seeking.
Responsive design has significant benefits, as you may:

  • Reach a larger audience;
  • Save time and development efforts;
  • Ensure design consistency across devices.

The use of graphical and interaction elements

In Human–Computer Interaction, responsive design is not only about screen size, but about adapting the interface to users, contexts, and devices. Graphical and interaction elements play a central role in this adaptation, as the use of those elements, including colours, is made according to standards and common bahaviours.
